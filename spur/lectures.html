SciTech Majlis

SPUR launched Scitech Majlis on 28th September 2012. These will be weekly group discussion sessions about science and technology along with talks by students. The primary goal of such sessions is to bring together people with common field of interest and promote learning through peer discussions. Professors, UG students and PG students will be invited to helm these discussions.

Session #1 : Talk by Arpan Saha (4th year UG, Physics) on "Blackholes: cold or hot?"

The talk was held on 28th September in GG 103. It went on for about 100 minutes and was attended by a very diverse audience of around 40 including freshies, senior UGs and DoSA, Prof. Yajnik!

Talks as part of UG Research Symposium:

As part of the Symposium, 3 talks by professors were organised. All 3 talks were held in IRCC auditorium.

  1. Prof. Milind Sohoni
    2. Computer Science and Engineering Department and Head, CTARA - Centre for Technology Alternatives for Rural Areas, IIT Bombay

    Time: 3.30 - 4.30 pm
    Title: "Science and Technology: Development, Culture and Politics"
    About the talk:

    Prof. Sohoni believes that the role of an Engineering college should be that of a regional solution provider. In the talk, he analyzed the nature of Research and Development (R&D) as it is practiced in IITs and its effect on India's development. The revolutionary talk left a lasting impression on the audience!

  2. Prof. R.B. Sunoj
    3. Chemistry Department

    Time: 4.45 - 5.45 pm
    Title: "Why should you do research?"
    About the talk:

    Prof. Sunoj is a proponent of UG Research. In the talk, he analyzed the importance of research for a UG, through amazing illustrations and very lucid examples, sprinkled with humour, which made the talk very relatable to the students.

  3. An interactive session with Prof. V.M. Gadre
    4. Electrical Engineering Department

    Time: 6.00 - 7.00 pm
    Title: "From UG to a Professor"
    About the talk:

    Prof. Gadre went down the memory lane and recounted his days as a UG student in IIT Delhi to a Professor in IIT Bombay. He answered various queries of students about research and academics. His down-to-earth nature made the talk very memorable one.
